Problem statement
Need statement
Jews worldwide have been facing unprecedented levels of antisemitism since the start of the COVID-19 pandemic in both cyber and public spaces.
Countries worldwide need to work on better protecting their Jewish citizens against antisemitism by putting in place measures to educate their populations on the issue and prevent the spread of antisemitic rhetoric and violence.


U.S. Jews:
  • 2,172 reported incidents of assault, vandalism, and harassment in 2021
  • 61% increase in incidents at Jewish institutions (525)
  • 3/4 of American Jews believe there is more U.S. antisemitism today than 5 years ago
  • The most targeted religious group in the U.S.
Worldwide:
  • World Zionist Organization & Jewish Agency reported an average of 10 antisemitic incidents a day in 2021 (likely underreported)
  • 2021 was the most antisemitic year in the last decade
  • Europe: 50% of the global total
  • UK: 1,308 reported incidents
  • Germany: 1,850 reported incidents
Progress being made:
  • Many countries adopting the IHRA definition of antisemitism
  • Holocaust education laws passed
  • Laws preventing the use of Holocaust symbols
