Broader Historical Context
Claim: Famous antisemitic conspiracy theories have continued to be perpetuated throughout the centuries, leading societies to blame the Jews for the spread of deadly diseases.
Implications: By debunking these conspiracy theories, in the future extremists will hold less influence when trying to blame the Jews for the world's various problems and people will be more equipped with stopping the spread of misinformation and antisemitism.
14th Century Jews were blamed for spreading the Black Plague. Christians claimed that the Jews had poisoned the wells.
  • Explanation: This conspiracy is continued to be believed by thousands around the world. Now, many are drawing parallels from this misinformation to the Covid pandemic and blaming Jews for it.
Jewish doctors in the Soviet Union were accused and arrested for allegedly trying to poison Stalin and Soviet leadership.
  • Explanation: This shows that since the middle ages people continued to blame Jews for the deaths or sickness of others.
